BitPack

Year
2025
Role
Designer / Course Project (INDN 301)
Tools
Persona Research, Survey Analysis, CAD Pattern Development

Overview

BitPack is a backpack concept designed for the modern hybrid tech worker — someone who splits their day between a co-working space, a laptop bag, and a life outside of work, and whose current bag isn't really built for any of those at once. The goal was a bag that balances work and leisure without forcing a tradeoff between the two.

Design brief

I built the brief around a persona: Alex Carter, 37, a software engineer living in a high-density San Francisco neighborhood, relying on public transit and walking rather than a car. Alex commutes daily to a co-working space, occasionally works from cafes or parks, and consistently carries a laptop, tablet, noise-canceling headphones, and a reusable bottle. Alex wants a minimalist, modern look, but won't sacrifice functionality to get it.

To pressure-test that persona, I ran a survey asking people directly what they wished their backpack had that it didn't. The recurring themes shaped the brief as much as the persona did:

  • "I wish the fabric was easier to clean"
  • "More space / a deeper pocket"
  • "Storage that was all contained within one large compartment..."
  • "Make a backpack for designers..."

Process

From the persona and survey data, I worked through overall proportions and compartment sizing to fit a laptop, tablet, and daily-carry items without the bag ballooning into an oversized commuter pack — dimensioning each panel and pocket against real device sizes rather than guessing at proportions. That sizing pass fed directly into the pattern development for the bag's construction.

  • Persona development grounded in real commuting and work habits, not assumptions
  • User survey used to validate and refine feature priorities before committing to a form
  • Dimensioned panel layout sized around laptop, tablet, and daily-carry essentials
  • Design language kept minimalist and modern per persona preference, without cutting functional storage

Outcome

The final deliverable was a fully dimensioned backpack concept addressing the top pain points identified in research: easy-to-clean materials, consolidated main storage instead of scattered small pockets, and enough capacity for a genuinely mobile workday. The project reinforced how much a straightforward persona-plus-survey process can sharpen a brief before a single line is drawn.

Tools used: Persona and survey research to define requirements, dimensioned CAD pattern layout for construction planning.
