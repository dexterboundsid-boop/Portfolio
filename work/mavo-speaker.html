Mavo

Year
2025
Role
Designer / Course Project (INDN 302)
Tools
Fusion 360, 3D Printing

Overview

Mavo is a minimalist portable speaker built around one idea: strip a speaker down to the controls that actually matter, then make those controls satisfying to use. Intuitive volume control, an adjustable kickstand for aiming sound precisely, and an integrated hook for hanging it wherever it's needed — no menus, no unnecessary buttons.

Design brief

The brief was simple on paper and harder in practice: design a speaker that blends into any environment instead of demanding attention, without sacrificing function. That meant working through the form, detail, and finish as three separate problems — simple shapes and rounded edges for the exterior, a hi-tech, transparent, detailed interior visible through the housing, and a clean, smooth surface finish that ties it all together.

Process

One of the more distinctive decisions was the surface texture: I gave Mavo a marble-like finish so the product could be handled with dirty or greasy hands — the kind of hands it's actually going to be picked up with in a workshop or kitchen — without showing marks the way a plain white surface would.

The name follows the same logic as the material. Mavo is a combination of "Marble" and the Latin word "Novo," meaning new, or not historic — a nod to taking a familiar material association and putting it in a genuinely new context.

  • Full CAD model and mechanism design for the adjustable kickstand in Fusion 360
  • Marble-textured surface treatment developed for grip and smudge resistance
  • Integrated hook detail for hanging storage or in-use positioning
  • Iteration on volume control interaction for one-handed, intuitive use

Outcome

Mavo embraces minimalist design with intuitive volume control for effortless use. The adjustable kickstand gives full control over the speaker's angle, directing sound precisely, while the integrated hook offers versatile hanging options for any space. The result is a speaker designed for both function and style — built to blend seamlessly into whatever environment it ends up in.

Tools used: Fusion 360 for CAD, mechanism, and surface modeling; 3D printing for prototyping the marble-textured housing and kickstand mechanism.
